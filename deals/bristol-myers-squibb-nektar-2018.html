Bristol-Myers Squibb entered a global clinical collaboration with Nektar Therapeutics to evaluate combinations of BMS's immuno-oncology portfolio (including Opdivo and Yervoy) with Nektar's NKTR-214 (bempegaldesleukin), a CD122-biased IL-2 pathway agonist. BMS paid $1.85B upfront ($1B cash + $850M equity investment) with up to $1.78B in additional milestone payments.
