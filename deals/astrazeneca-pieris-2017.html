AstraZeneca and Pieris entered a strategic collaboration to develop inhaled Anticalin-based respiratory therapies. Pieris received $45M upfront plus $12.5M for Phase 1 initiation of PRS-060 (~$57.5M near-term), with potential for up to $2.1B in development and commercial milestones plus tiered royalties. AstraZeneca funds all development; Pieris retains U.S. co-development/co-commercialization option from Phase 2a.

Did it work? Outcome assessment

Not actually an acquisition — a $2.1B respiratory Anticalin collaboration whose lead asset failed toxicology and was fully terminated by AstraZeneca in 2023, gutting Pieris (70% layoffs) and contributing to its eventual reverse-merger exit.
